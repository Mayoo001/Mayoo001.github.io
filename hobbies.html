• Running


    At the start of 2020 I started my running journey, at first I found incredibly difficult and a big commitment, in fact you could probably say I hated the activity. As I began to do it more often it not only became easier but it also gave me a sense of freedom as if my problems were just magically gone. Since then running has been my personal therapy and actually helped me feel better and healthier. It has also led to other activities that are meant for my well being. `

  • Weightlifting


    Similar to running I began weighlifiting as a commitment to myself and my body. At first just entering a gym was scary and intimidating but a friend really helped me get over the fear by coaching me through workouts. As I began to see progress this and experience growth this motivated me to become the best version of myself I could be. As I look back on my progress over the years it has made me extremely happy to see the improvements I have made for myself and the results of consistency and hardwork. For this reason weightlifting is currently my most prominent hobby and one that I will continue to do forever.

  • Skateboarding


    Skateboarding is an individualistic pastime I have enjoyed from my teens. It has allowed me to learn how to form communities around activities I like doing. Although I don't often skate anymore it really taught me about passion and what we are capable of doing if we set our minds to it.
